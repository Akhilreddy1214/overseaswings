The University was founded in its current avatar in 2004 through the fusion of the UMIST (University of Manchester Institute of Science and Technology) and the Victoria University of Manchester. After collaborating for 100 years, the institutions merged on 22nd October 2004, creating the biggest single-site UK University.

The University of Manchester in the UK is located towards the southern end of the city center in Manchester, encompassing Oxford Road and Sackville Street, the earlier homes of the Victoria University and UMIST respectively. The University of Manchester is a public research university known for its checkered history. Around 25 Nobel Prize winners have studied or worked at the institution while the atom was first split here by Ernest Rutherford. Alan Turing invented artificial intelligence here while the first stored-program computer in the world was made at the university. The university is known for its Square Kilometer Array, the biggest radio telescope in the world, and also the Jodrell Bank observatory with its Lovell Telescope.

The University of Manchester ranking is 1 in THE (Times Higher Education) University Impact Rankings and 27 (World) in the QS World University Rankings for 2021. It is ranked at 35 in the World in the Academic Rankings of World Universities (2021). THE has ranked it at 50 (World) in its 2022 rankings.

Test requirements

For undergraduate course applicants whose first language is not English, proficiency must be proved via a recognized English Language test. This could be IELTS, TOEFL, or an English qualification like Norwegian Vitnemal or Jong Kong O-level. The tests should be taken two years before applying to the University of Manchester.
The minimum qualifying score for IELTS is 6.0 (or equivalent), and the score in the individual component must not go below 5.5. Certain subjects may require a higher score than others.
Under general tests for postgraduate course applicants comes proof of English Language proficiency. Though the tests and average scores may vary among departments, they are usually equivalent to the International English Language Testing System (IELTS), with average scores ranging from 6.0 to 7.5.
